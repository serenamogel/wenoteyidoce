Geno Smith biography and early life

American football quarterback, Eugene Cyril “Geno” Smith III was born on October 10th, 1990 in Miramar. Florida in the United States of America.

Smith was accepted into the magnet program at Norland Middle School, where two hours a day were devoted to arts education. Damon Cogdell, a former Mountaineer, served as his coach while he was a student at Miramar High School.

His 2,200 passing yards, 25 touchdown passes, and three interceptions as a junior earned him a spot on the second team of all-state quarterbacks. As an athlete for 2007, he was also chosen to the second team all-Broward County.

He received an invitation to the exclusive Elite 11 quarterback camp in Aliso Viejo, California, after his junior year.

Smith’s senior year saw him guide his squad to the state 6A semifinals while also running for more than 300 yards and completing 205 of 338 passes for 3,089 yards and 30 touchdowns.

He was a Florida Class 6A first-team all-state pick and a Parade All-American. The Miami Herald and South Florida Sun Sentinel recognized him as the best player in Broward County, and he came in second place in the Mr. Florida vote.

He was included among ESPN’s Top 150 prospects and concluded his career as the third-best passer in Broward County history. Notwithstanding offers from Florida State, South Florida, Boston College, and Alabama, he decided to attend West Virginia.

Geno Smith career

Before being selected by the New York Jets in the second round of the 2013 NFL Draft, Smith played college football for West Virginia, where he helped the Mountaineers win many bowl games, set various passing records, and win numerous honors.

Smith spent his final two years with the Jets as the team’s starting quarterback after two disappointing seasons, but injuries ultimately cost him the job.

He later served as a backup quarterback for the Seattle Seahawks, New York Giants, and Los Angeles Chargers before unpredictably reviving his career in 2022 as the team’s starter, making his first Pro Bowl appearance, posting his first winning record and making the playoffs as a starter, and ultimately taking home the Comeback Player Award.
