Allie LaForce | Career

Beauty Pageants

Allie LaForce experienced success at a very early age. Her career took a graph after winning a beauty pageant. First, she participated in Miss Teen Ohio USA in 2005 and won that beauty pageant.

After winning Miss Teen Ohio, she participated in another beauty pageant (Miss Teen USA). She was representing Ohio. She didn’t win the Miss Teen USA title, but she was one of the titleholders.

Miss Teen Ohio got many opportunities; she got a one-year modeling contract with Trump Modeling Management and scholarships to schools for films and television in New York City.

She also traveled a lot to promote awareness about drugs and alcohol consumption and also managed to continue with her studies as well.

Sideline Journalism

LaForce majored in broadcast journalism. During the 2010 Mid-American conference, she worked as the sideline reporter for Sportstime Ohio. Her journey started there, and she never had to look back again.

As a reporter and sports anchor, she signed up with Cleveland’s Fox affiliate, WJW channel 8, after college.

She also worked as a sideline reporter for NBA TV for NBA Summer League 2014.

It was also announced that LaForce might replace Tracy Wolfson as a CBS sideline reporter for the SEC. Currently, she is working for Turner Sports as a sideline reporter.

“I’m so excited for the opportunity to fulfill my lifelong passion and dream of covering basketball for a living,” LaForce said in a release.

Allie is now back in her work post-COVID-19. She explains her recent game experience as a once-in-a-lifetime experience because it was challenging, exciting, and unpredictable.

LaForce also thanks NBA for ensuring everyone’s safety and giving her a pleasant working environment during this pandemic.

It was reported on June 2018 that LaForce would leave CBS Sports as the two sides could not agree on a new deal.

LaForce still occasionally appears on CBS due to her March Madness reporting role. Turner Sports signed a deal with her to continue her role as a sideline reporter for the NBA on TNT and the NCAA Tournament.

Allie LaForce | Disease, Huntington

Everything seems good, healthy, and content when we look at the power couple Allie LaForce and Joe Smith’s lifestyle.

TNT reporter Allie married Joe Smith in 2015, and they became a major part of her life, but sadly, she became a part of the family battle as well, Huntington’s disease.

Doctors say it is rare and inherited, and the sufferers have a 50% chance of passing it down to their children. It causes the breakdown of nerve cells in the brain and has no cure to date.

Smith’s grandmother lost her life years ago because of the same disease, and his mother, Lee, was diagnosed in 2012.
