What is Roy Clark’s Net Worth?

Roy Clark, an American country musician, had a net worth of $10 million at the time of his death in 2018. Throughout his career, he achieved success as a musician, television personality, and actor. Clark was best known for co-hosting the television variety show “Hee Haw” from 1969 to 1993, alongside Buck Owens. He showcased his exceptional skills on the guitar, banjo, and fiddle. In addition to his work on “Hee Haw,” Clark also frequently served as a guest host for Johnny Carson on “The Tonight Show” during the 1960s and 1970s. He received numerous accolades for his contributions to the country music industry throughout his lifetime, including induction into the Grand Ole Opry and the Country Music Hall of Fame.

Early Life

Roy Clark was born on April 15, 1933, in Meherrin, Virginia. He grew up in a musical household, as both of his parents played instruments. Clark developed a passion for music at a young age and began playing the guitar when he was 14. He quickly learned how to play the banjo and mandolin and won two National Banjo Championships by the time he was 15. His talent and love for music propelled him into a successful career in the entertainment industry.

Career

Clark’s career in music began with performances in the Washington, DC area, where he partnered with fellow guitarist Carl Lukat. He made his television debut on WTTG and later had the opportunity to perform at the Grand Ole Opry. Clark toured the country as a backup guitarist for various acts before returning to his local music scene and recording singles for various record labels.

In 1954, he joined Jimmy Dean’s band, the Texas Wildcats, as their lead guitarist. Clark gained exposure through his appearances on Dean’s radio and television shows. He also competed on “Arthur Godfrey’s Talent Scouts” and came in second place. Clark continued to make his mark in the entertainment industry, appearing on various shows and collaborating with renowned artists like Wanda Jackson.

By the 1970s, Clark had become one of the highest-paid country music stars in the country. He recorded several hit country songs throughout the decade and continued to release music well into the 80s and 90s. Clark also ventured into other forms of entertainment, including hosting and acting on television shows like “Hee Haw,” “The Tonight Show,” “The Beverly Hillbillies,” and “The Odd Couple.”

Personal Life and Death

In 1957, Clark married Barbara Rupard, and they had five children together. They resided in Tulsa, Oklahoma. Clark was known for his love of flying and owned several planes. Tragically, he passed away in November of 2018 at the age of 85 from complications of pneumonia.
