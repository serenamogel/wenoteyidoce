Net Worth, Songs and Movies

Of all his acting performances, Louie received the most accolades for his depiction of the Jamaican Kingpin in the 1998 hood classic “Belly.” He played the character Ox with the rappers DMX and Nas.

Reuniting with Nas in 2016, he made a cameo on DJ Khaled’s “Nas Album Done” music video.

“Shottas” and “We Run These Streets” were Louie’s other unforgettable films.

Owned by his filmography, Screen Actors Guild granted him membership.

His musical achievements, more than his acting, characterized him. The self-proclaimed “Original Don Dada” was instrumental in taking dance hall and reggaeton sound to the mainstream.

In addition to his 1992 Grammy-winning single “Typewriter,” Louie’s discography included genre-defining songs such as “The Muscle,” “The Sting” and “Poison.”
