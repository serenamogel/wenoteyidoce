Arnold Palmer: Investments and Endorsements

Investments

Palmer was the owner of a range of enterprises, many of which were golf-related.

In Orlando, Florida, he owned the Bay Hill Club and Lodge.

He also owned the Latrobe Country Club, which he bought in 1971.

Arnold was a founding member of the sports network The Golf Channel.

Palmer Course Design

Likewise, he also owned Palmer Course Design.

Palmer Course Design was founded in 1972 by him and his design associate Ed Seay.

The company was rebranded as Arnold Palmer Design Company in 2006.

Palmer worked as a designer on over 300 golf courses in 37 states, 25 countries, and five continents.

This includes China’s first modern golf course, which was established in 1988.

Car Business

Palmer also got into the car business in 1974.

He bought a Cadillac dealership in Charlotte, North Carolina.

In this venture, he partnered with Mark McCormack and Don Massey.

He also owned several other dealerships in different states.

In his hometown, he established Arnold Palmer Motors, which was a Buick-Cadillac dealership.

After 36 years in service, the Latrobe business closed in 2017.

Arnold Palmer Enterprises oversaw all of Palmer’s sponsorship, trademarks, spokesperson deals, and commercial partnerships.

Arnold Palmer: Career

Palmer learned the game from his father, who was the head professional and greenskeeper at Latrobe Country Club.

He then joined the United States Coast Guard, where he served from 1951 until 1954.

After his Coast Guard stint finished, he returned to college and competitive golfing.

Arnold Palmer eventually won seven major titles throughout his remarkable career.

He won four Masters, two Open Championships, and one U.S. Open.

The 1960 U.S. Open at Cherry Hills was probably the most significant milestone in Arnold Palmer’s career, as he won his first and only U.S. Open Championship.

The Arnold Palmer Invitational, a PGA Tour event that Palmer began hosting in 1979 was renamed in his honor in 2007.

Palmer made a significant impression on the PGA Tour when he won the 1955 Canadian Open in his first year.

It would be the first of his 62 PGA Tour victories, putting him fifth all-time in that category.

Later that decade, at the 1958 Masters, ‘The King’ would win his first major championship.

He established himself as a celebrity not just in the world of golf but also in the world of sports, which was a remarkable accomplishment at the time.

FAQs

How did Arnold Palmer come up with his umbrella logo?

In fact, it’s a pretty interesting story. Palmer stepped outdoors on that rainy day to regroup, frustrated by his inability to come up with a logo idea. He saw a woman opening a multi-colored umbrella, it sparked him, and he proposed that as the logo.

Who inherited Arnold Palmer’s wealth?

Palmer’s widow, Kathleen Palmer, received a $10 million inheritance. His extensive estate, which includes many residences and golf courses, will be split evenly between his two daughters, Peggy and Amy Palmer.

Did Arnold Palmer remarry after the death of Winnie?

Winnie died at the age of 65 after a 45-year marriage. Palmer married Kathleen Gawthrop, his second wife.
