Hugo Lloris has established himself as one of the best goalkeepers over the last couple of years. His maturity and composure combined by his strong personality and leadership skills have made him a standout player in his club and the national side. He serves as the captain of the France national team and Tottenham Hotspur. His soft-spoken and humble persona off the pitch also make him a perfect role model for the younger generation.

    Hugo Lloris Facts

  • At the age of 6, he started playing as a goalkeeper. He played in that position continuously for two years. Then, for some time, he switched between striker and goalkeeper before settling on for keeper’s role for good after joining Nice’s academy.
  • While growing up, he excelled in tennis and was considered as one of the finest talents in the youth circuit. However, at the age of 13, he decided to give up tennis and focus solely on soccer.
  • He started playing soccer at the age of 6 at CEDAC (Centre de Diffusion et d’Action Culturelle), which is a cultural center based in Nice that offers a range of activities including soccer.
  • In August 2010, it was revealed that he would be featured alongside Karim Benzema on the cover of the FIFA 11’s French version.
  • Hugo was recommended to Nice by Dominique Baratelli, the former French international and ONG Nice goalkeeper. He was signed by the Nice’s academy at the age of 10.
  • In his debut season in 2005-06, he delivered great performances to help his side reach their first-ever Coupe de la Ligue final with tremendous performances in quarter-finals and semi-finals.
  • In the summer of 2008, he was heavily courted by the European heavyweights such as AC Milan and Tottenham Hotspur. However, he decided to join Lyon as he felt he would get opportunities to play more regularly with the French club.
  • Milan vice-president, Adriano Galliani, later revealed that they practically sealed Lloris’s move to AC Milan, which was only scuppered by Christian Abbiati’s refusal to move to Palermo.
  • In his debut season with Lyon, Hugo finished with 16 clean sheets, which was the 2nd best in the league. He was selected as the best goalkeeper in the league and included in Team of the Year as well.
  • In December 2009, he finished in the 3rd position behind Nicolas Anelka and Yoann Gourcuff in a voting conducted by France Football for the French Player of the Year award.
  • At the end of 2009-10 season, he was recognized as UNFP Ligue 1 Goalkeeper of the Year. This was his 2nd consecutive win.
  • In 2010, he was nominated for the UEFA Club Goalkeeper of the Year. However, he lost out to Júlio César, who had played a key role in Internazionale’s UEFA Champions League title.
  • In August 2012, he was purchased from Lyon by Tottenham Hotspur in a deal worth €10 million with further €5 million tied up in add-ons. Spurs also agreed to give Lyon 20% of Lloris’s future transfer profit.
  • His Premier League debut for Spurs ended American keeper Brad Friedel’s incredible run of 310 consecutive games in the Premier League.
  • In November 2013, he lost consciousness after colliding with Romelu Lukaku‘s knee in a match against Everton. However, he still completed the match. This led to FIFA and FIFPro, the head injury charities, severely criticizing Spurs manager André Villas-Boas for continuing with Lloris even after his collision.
  • In August 2015, Tottenham manager Mauricio Pochettino made Lloris the permanent captain of the team.
  • In December 2016, Lloris signed a 6 years contract extension with the London based club to extend his stay at the club until 2022. This helped club in avoiding Manchester United’s interest in the following summer transfer season as they sought to replace David de Gea, who was on a verge of the transfer to Real Madrid.
  • In June 2018, he made his 100th appearance for France in a FIFA World Cup group stage match against Peru. His side managed to keep the clean sheet in the 1-0 victory.
